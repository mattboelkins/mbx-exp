Subsection1.2.1Things that are automatically knowl-ized

Footnotes are even better as knowls. 1 Because they appear adjacent to the footnote in the text when you click on them.

If we do a theorem that has a proof, the proof is automatically in a knowl. The theorem structure in MBX also has several key levels/components: theorem, title, index, statement, proof.

Theorem1.2.1My Theorem

If a triangle has sides of length \(a\), \(b\), and \(c\), and \(a^2 + b^2 = c^2\), then the triangle is a right triangle.

Proof

Here is my insightful argument.

Examples are also automatically put into knowls.

Example1.2.2

Here is an example application of Theorem 1.2.1. Suppose that we have a triangle whose sides are of length 5, 12, 13. Then, since \(5^2 + 12^2 = 25 + 144 = 169 = 13^2\), it follows that the triangle must be a right triangle.
